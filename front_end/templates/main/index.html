Potassium keeps water and electrolyte balance in balance, Regulates blood pressure and is important for a regular heart attack and nerves.

To a potassium deficiency, it can cause vomiting after large losses of fluid, eg by prolonged diarrheal diseases Or prolonged use of drainage products.

A high potassium content in the blood can lead to heart failure Because potassium Inhibits the contraction of the heart muscle.

Calcium is needed for the formation of bones and teeth. Also for the muscles and clotting Calcium is important.

At calcium is often lacking, which can lead to a decrease in bone strength and thus to osteoporosis.

Magnesium affects the energy metabolism, the interaction of nerves to muscles for protein synthesis and and bone mineralization. Furthermore, it is responsible for the activation of over 300 enzymes in the metabolism.

Too much magnesium is highly unlikely. In large quantities of mineral causes a softer stool.

Chloride is a component of table salt and is therefore responsible for the regulation of water balance. Also for the nerves, the heart and muscles, as well as acid-base balance chloride is important.

Since it occurs in many foods, more than enough chloride is usually added. A permanent oversupply of chloride can cause high blood pressure.

Nitrate is converted to nitrite in the body toxic. In addition, may arise from nitrites carcinogenic nitrosamines.

The nitrite itself may cause an oxygen deficiency of all organs and tissues. Infants are particularly vulnerable for this.

Therefore, the limit value in mineral water is 50 mg / l. The nitrate levels in drinking water contained in the region are having 5 to 38 mg / l below the limit and are therefore classified as harmless.

For comparison: Bodensee-water contains only 4 mg of nitrate per liter.

Where does our drinking water?

In the city district, there are about 40 springs and wells, district is around 130th

Since the Heilbronner water but charged very hard and strong with toxic nitrate, buys the city for 43 years of water from Lake Constance (Bodensee Zweckverband water supply).

Previously, the city covered the water needs about 60 to 70 percent self-sufficient water. This has turned into its opposite. The proportion of water from Lake Constance is now at 80 to 85 percent.

Why miss some municipalities on the addition of water from Lake Constance?

It is cheaper for local authorities when they take their drinking water exclusively from its own sources.

The municipality Jagsthausen example provides its residents with 100 percent self-sufficient water.

"We have ordinary water, which is hard indeed. But we see no reason for an admixture of extraneous water as do other municipalities "

Mayor Roland Holder

Complaints from the community there would be no, many residents have a water softener. Links to the Bodensee water is connected in the case of Jagsthausen with high investment costs and not an issue.

Why does not taste as good as my drinking bottled water?

The taste of water is determined by the minerals contained in it.

Water with much sodium and potassium, is perceived as salty taste. Sodium-poor water - including Heilbronn drinking water - contrast tastes moderates.

An unpleasant taste can occur when the pipes are contaminated in the building. Stadtwerke check the quality of drinking water only up to the respective front door. In the house, the responsibility rests with the building owner.

What is the hardness of water for me?

Wherever hard water flows slowly or is heated to about 60 degrees, lime settles. In the kitchen and bathroom so arise lime stains, washing clothes more detergent is needed, some household appliances must be decalcified regularly. This is from a water hardness of 21st

In Heilbronn, the upper limit of the water hardness of 18 degrees. Healthwise hard water is safe to the contrary, because of the high calcium and magnesium content, it is healthier than soft water.

Why is there no soft water with us?

Most of Heilbronner drinking fountains are located in the rock layer Gipskeuper, who is responsible for the very high magnesium and calcium content in the water. Since these two minerals determine the hardness of the water, there are in Heilbronn soft water.

The Heilbronner water has a hardness of up to 28 degrees of German hardness, in some of the 40 well water flows at 30 degrees German hardness.

How do the different degrees of hardness?

The taste of water is determined by the minerals contained in it.

Drinking water containing varying amounts of the minerals calcium and magnesium - depending on whether it is surface water, ground water or spring water.

The more calcium and magnesium in the water, the harder it is.

Unit ° dH - "degree of German hardness"?

The unit ° dH (German hardness) is outdated, but in practice common still. In addition ° dH millimoles per liter (mmol / l) was used.

Industries:

  • soft: less than 1.5 millimoles of calcium carbonate per liter (equivalent to less than 8.4 degrees of German hardness)
  • medium: 1.5 to 2.5 millimoles of calcium carbonate per liter (equivalent to 8.4 to 14 degrees of German hardness)
  • hard: more than 2.5 millimoles of calcium carbonate per liter (equivalent to more than 14 degrees of German hardness)

As the water-Tool has arisen

Drinking water is considered the best controlled food in Germany. And yet we know very little about the water that comes out of our taps. Many municipalities publish the study results on its websites. these data are available for the citizens - but not tangible. What does a hardness of 9? Are 200 milligrams calcium per liter lot or a little? How rich in minerals is our tap water, can only be seen when we compare it with water from the market.

This is now possible for the region Heilbronn. The idea came in late February at the Open Data Day in Heilbronn. Although many data were available online, they had to be placed in a machine-readable form first. Many municipalities only publish PDF files with the annual averages. These are not machine-readable, and had to be carried by the editors. To program the application, a single record is necessary. On some websites of municipalities the information sought in the drinking water were hidden on sub-pages, outdated or incomplete. In many towns and cities, we have therefore requested the data via email. Only three municipalities came so far no response.

It was surprising to us how different the drinking water is composed in the region. Mostly the mineral content within the village differs greatly - namely when there are different service areas. Municipalities as Bad Friedrichshall, Neckarsulm or Zaberfeld are divided into several areas. This was a particular challenge in the data collection phase. The areas had partly be assigned to the roads, so that the user gets the correct values ​​for its own road appears. The drinking water prices offers the Statistical Office a complete data set for all counties - for drinking water quality, there is such public record does not exist.

The application of these zugrundliegende record was therefore a complex research project. Not less burdensome were implementation and visualization for the developer Felix Ebert, Ulrich Holly and Tom Goerner. A large role was played by user-friendliness and design. From dry figures an interactive graphic to be built, through which the user gets access to his drinking. For the graphical elements Heiko was not responsible.

The water application is a beta version. In particular, the data collection can never be completed. Many municipalities publish soon the current average annual values ​​- they should also be updated in our application. We are dependent on the cooperation of the town halls and have set up a contact region. Due to the large data set may cause erroneous information. Who discovered Auffälligkeifen can contact us anytime. We welcome all feedback.

Data and code of the application are open to the public and available under an open source license.

You're interested in more open data applications or even want to participate? In Heilbronn we meet regularly in the context of code for Germany to jointly implement open data projects - we would be pleased about your visit!
